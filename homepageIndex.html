About Us Meet your hosts

Dating back nearly 2 years ago, three buddies found a common bond in the unlikeliest of places: a foosball table. Their new cohabitation led to an unexpected kinship fostered by their shared passion for the game. Each evening, amidst the clatter of spinning rods and the cheers of victory, they found a camaraderie that transcended mere roommates. With every intense match, the clinks and clatters of the foosball game acted as a backdrop to their growing friendship. The competitive banter and mutual respect that developed during these games became the foundation of their housemate brotherhood, solidifying their bond beyond just shared walls and chores.

John Marion

Tournament Rules Check 'em out

In the thrilling realm of foosball, adherence to key rules is essential for fair and exhilarating gameplay. One paramount regulation, often emphasized, is the prohibition of spinning the rods. This rule stands as a cornerstone, emphasizing the necessity of controlling the movement of the rods without employing spinning techniques. Players must maneuver the rods skillfully, employing wrist flicks and strategic placements to navigate the ball. This restriction on spinning ensures a level playing field, promoting skillful, calculated gameplay and preventing unfair advantages, ultimately contributing to an engaging and competitive environment for all participants.

For a complete list of rules, please see the ITSF Rule Book
